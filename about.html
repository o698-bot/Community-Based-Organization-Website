About Us

One Touch ICT was officially registered in 2018 as a Community-Based Organization. The initiative began as a small ICT training center in Mathare, offering free computer literacy and digital skills training to community members. This early focus aimed to bridge the digital divide and equip residents—especially young people—with basic computer skills to improve their employability.

Over time, we recognized that ICT skills alone were not sufficient to address the broader economic challenges facing the community. In response, the organization expanded its programming to include a range of vocational and technical skills training designed to better meet local needs and enhance income-generation opportunities for youth.

Today, One Touch ICT offers diverse empowerment programs that combine digital literacy with practical vocational skills, supporting participants to transition into employment, entrepreneurship, and self-reliance.

To date, the organization has directly benefited over 10,000 individuals through its various programs, contributing to improved livelihoods, increased opportunities, and stronger community resilience in Mathare.
