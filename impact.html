Stories of our impact

Brian Mutua

"The business plan training helped me see my farming idea as something real, not just a dream."

Brian spent four years as a boda boda rider before enrolling in our entrepreneurship track. Combining what he already knew about logistics with newly acquired financial management skills, he launched a vegetable aggregation venture linking small-scale farmers in Machakos to supermarkets in Nairobi.

Faith Atieno

"My mentor never let me quit. When I failed my first exam she called me the same evening."

Faith was paired with a mentor in our one-year mentorship programme while studying bookkeeping on the side. Her mentor, a senior finance officer, guided her through certification exams and helped her negotiate her first formal employment offer. She is now a full-time accounts assistant at a logistics firm in Kisumu and mentors two women in the current cohort.

James Kariuki

"I had a degree and no job for two years. The internship placement changed everything in six weeks."

James graduated with a computer science diploma but could not secure employment without experience. Our job linkage programme placed him in a six-week internship at a mid-sized ICT firm in Thika. He was absorbed as a permanent staff member before the internship ended. He now earns three times the national minimum wage and has moved his family from a single room into a two-bedroom apartment
